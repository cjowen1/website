Scientific Highlights

  • 03/2025: New preprint: "Revealing the proton slingshot mechanism in solid acid electrolytes through machine learning molecular dynamics." This work reveals the proton slingshot effect in solid-acid electrolyte materials using machine learned force fields. This challenges long-held assumptions of the mechanism and allows for the development of design rules for increasing the conductivity of these systems.
  • 07/2024: New preprint: "Surface roughening in nanoparticle catalysts." This work challenges the assumed surface structure of nanocatalysts using machine learned force fields. Such computational methods are proven to be capable of decoupling disparate experimental observables, allowing for more direct understanding of such dynamic phenomena.
  • 07/2024: New preprint: "Atomistic evolution of active sites in multi-component heterogeneous catalysts." This work provides a machine learned force field that can directly probe the deactivation mechanism of bimetallic catalysts, and provide more accessible insight into applicable design rules.
  • 05/2024: New preprint: "Thermodynamically informed multimodal learning of high-dimensional free energy models in molecular coarse graining." This work presents a differentiable formalism for learning free energies that is capable of capturing arbitrarily complex model dependencies on coarse-grained coordinates and finite-temperature response to variation of general system parameters.
  • 05/2024: New publication: "Complexity of many-body interactions in transition metals via machine-learned force fields from the TM23 data set." This work introduces TM23, a new ML potential benchmark of transition metals and test the performance of NequIP and FLARE.
  • 05/2024: New publication: "Low-index mesoscopic surface reconstructions of Au surfaces using Bayesian force fields." This work enables large-scale molecular dynamics simulations to describe the thermodynamics and time evolution of the low-index mesoscopic surface reconstructions of gold surfaces.
